# LinkedIn Content Templates

Use these templates to create fast first drafts, then personalize with user context.

## Post Frameworks

### 1) Problem -> Insight -> Action

- Hook: Name a painful, specific problem.
- Body: Explain why the common approach fails.
- Insight: Share the shift that works better.
- Action: End with a clear next step or CTA.

Template:

```text
[Pain statement]

Most people try [common approach], but it fails because [reason].

What works better: [new approach].

If you are [target user], try this today:
1) [step]
2) [step]
3) [step]

If helpful, I can share [resource/next step].
```

### 2) Story -> Lesson -> CTA

- Hook: Start with a concrete moment.
- Story: Give context, challenge, and decision.
- Lesson: State the transferable principle.
- CTA: Invite discussion or offer resource.

Template:

```text
Last [time period], I [specific situation].

I thought [initial belief], but [what happened].

The lesson: [principle].

Now I always [new behavior].

Have you seen this too in [domain]?
```

### 3) Contrarian Take -> Evidence -> Nuance

- Hook: Challenge a common belief.
- Evidence: Provide data, example, or direct observation.
- Nuance: Explain where it does and does not apply.
- CTA: Ask for counterpoints.

Template:

```text
Unpopular opinion: [contrarian statement].

Why: [evidence 1], [evidence 2], [evidence 3].

This does not mean [overreach].
It applies when [conditions].

Agree or disagree?
```

## Hook Bank

- "Most people get [outcome] wrong because they optimize for [wrong thing]."
- "I stopped doing [old tactic]. Results improved in [timeframe]."
- "One small change that made [metric] jump: [change]."
- "If I had to restart in [industry], I would do these 3 things first."
- "The advice that sounds smart but hurts [audience]: [advice]."

## First Comment Templates

Use the first comment to add depth without bloating the post.

```text
Extra context I could not fit in the post:
- [point]
- [point]
- [point]
```

```text
If you want the [checklist/template], comment "[keyword]" and I will share it.
```

## Comment Reply Templates

### Supportive reply

```text
Appreciate that. The part that made the biggest difference was [detail].
```

### Clarifying reply

```text
Great question. I mean [definition]. In practice, start with [first step].
```

### Skeptical reply

```text
Fair pushback. This approach works best when [conditions]. If those are missing, I would do [alternative].
```

## Connection Request Notes

Keep notes short and specific.

```text
Hi [Name], I enjoyed your post on [topic]. I work on [area] and would value connecting.
```

```text
Hi [Name], we both work in [domain]. Your take on [topic] stood out. Open to connect?
```

## Follow-Up DM Sequence

### Touch 1 (1-2 days after connect)

```text
Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I liked your point about [topic]. Curious: how are you approaching [specific question] this quarter?
```

### Touch 2 (3-5 days later)

```text
Sharing this in case useful: [resource]. It helped with [outcome]. No pressure, just thought it matched your work on [topic].
```

### Touch 3 (final nudge, 5-7 days later)

```text
Closing the loop here. If helpful, I can send a short breakdown of how we handle [problem]. If not relevant now, all good.
```
